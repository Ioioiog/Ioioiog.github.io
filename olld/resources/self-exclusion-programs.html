Self-Exclusion Programs

What are Self-Exclusion Programs?

Self-exclusion programs are voluntary initiatives that allow individuals to exclude themselves from gambling activities for a specified period. These programs are designed to help people who struggle with gambling addiction or want to take a break from gambling.

By enrolling in a self-exclusion program, you agree to be banned from entering gambling venues or accessing online gambling sites. This can be an effective tool in managing gambling habits and reducing the risk of problem gambling.

How Self-Exclusion Programs Work

  1. Voluntary enrollment: You choose to participate in the program.
  2. Set a time period: Decide how long you want to be excluded (e.g., 6 months, 1 year, 5 years, or lifetime).
  3. Provide personal information: Submit your details to be added to the exclusion list.
  4. Enforcement: Gambling venues and websites will deny you access during your exclusion period.
  5. Support: Many programs offer additional resources and counseling services.

Benefits of Self-Exclusion

  • Creates a barrier to gambling, reducing temptation
  • Provides time to seek help and develop coping strategies
  • Can help in repairing relationships damaged by gambling
  • Assists in regaining control over finances
  • Supports overall mental health and well-being

Types of Self-Exclusion Programs

1. Casino Self-Exclusion

Excludes you from entering physical casino properties.

2. Online Gambling Self-Exclusion

Blocks access to online gambling websites and apps.

3. State-Wide Self-Exclusion

Covers all gambling venues and online platforms within a specific state.

4. National Self-Exclusion

Applies to multiple states or countries, depending on the program.

How to Enroll in a Self-Exclusion Program

The process may vary depending on your location and the type of program, but generally involves these steps:

  1. Research available programs in your area
  2. Contact the relevant gambling authority or visit their website
  3. Fill out an application form
  4. Provide identification and a recent photograph
  5. Choose your exclusion period
  6. Review and sign the self-exclusion agreement

Remember, self-exclusion is a serious commitment. Once enrolled, it's typically not possible to reverse the decision until the chosen period has elapsed.
