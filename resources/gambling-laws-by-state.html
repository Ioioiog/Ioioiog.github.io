International Gambling Laws

Gambling laws vary significantly around the world. This page provides an overview of gambling regulations across different regions and lists organizations that can help gamblers. Please note that gambling laws can change, and this information should not be considered legal advice.

Click on a region below to view specific gambling laws, regulations, and support organizations:

North America Gambling Laws and Support

Gambling laws in North America vary by country:

  • United States: Laws vary by state, with some allowing casinos, lotteries, and sports betting.
    • Nevada: Known for Las Vegas, allows most forms of gambling statewide.
    • New Jersey: Has a thriving casino industry in Atlantic City and legal online gambling.
    • Utah and Hawaii: Have complete bans on all forms of gambling.
  • Canada: Each province regulates gambling, with many forms legal across the country.
    • Ontario: Recently legalized online gambling, including sports betting.
    • British Columbia: Operates its own online casino and sports betting platform.
    • Quebec: Has a state-run online gambling site and several land-based casinos.
  • Mexico: Casino gambling is legal in licensed establishments, and there's a national lottery.
    • Land-based casinos are legal and regulated by the Ministry of the Interior.
    • Online gambling is in a legal grey area, with no specific laws prohibiting or regulating it.
    • Sports betting is legal and popular, especially for soccer matches.

Online gambling regulations differ significantly among these countries, with the U.S. having a patchwork of state laws, Canada allowing province-run online gambling, and Mexico having no clear regulations for online gambling.

Organizations that can help gamblers:

  • United States: National Council on Problem Gambling - www.ncpgambling.org
  • Canada: Canadian Centre on Substance Use and Addiction - www.ccsa.ca
  • Mexico: Centros de Integración Juvenil - www.cij.gob.mx

South America Gambling Laws and Support

Gambling laws in South America are evolving:

  • Brazil: Casino gambling is illegal, but some states allow for electronic gaming and betting.
  • Argentina: Each province regulates gambling, with various forms legal, including online gambling.
  • Chile: Licensed casinos operate legally, while online gambling laws are currently unclear.

Organizations that can help gamblers:

  • Brazil: Associação Brasileira de Jogo Legal - www.abjl.org.br
  • Argentina: Fundación de Ayuda al Problemático del Juego - www.fundacionjuego.org.ar
  • Chile: Santiago de Chile Gambling Addiction Help Center - www.addictionhelp.cl

Europe Gambling Laws and Support

European gambling laws are diverse:

  • United Kingdom: Has a well-regulated gambling industry, including online gambling.
  • France: Allows many forms of gambling, with strict regulations on online gambling.
  • Germany: Recently liberalized online gambling laws, with varying regulations by state.
  • Italy: Has a regulated gambling market, including online gambling.
  • Romania: Legalized online gambling in 2015, with a regulated market for both land-based and online operators.
  • Netherlands (Holland): Recently introduced regulations for online gambling, with a licensing system implemented in 2021.
  • Belgium: Has a strictly regulated gambling market, with licenses required for both online and land-based operations.
  • Austria: Allows various forms of gambling, with a mix of state monopoly and private operators in different sectors.

Organizations that can help gamblers:

  • United Kingdom: GamCare - www.gamcare.org.uk
  • France: SOS Joueurs - www.sosjoueurs.com
  • Germany: Deutsche Hauptstelle für Suchtfragen - www.dhs.de
  • Italy: Associazione Italiana Giochi - www.aigeonline.com
  • Romania: Responsible Gambling - jocresponsabil.ro
  • Netherlands: AGOG (Anonymous Gamblers) - www.agog.nl
  • Belgium: Gambling Commission - www.gamingcommission.be
  • Austria: Spielsuchthilfe - www.spielsuchthilfe.at

Asia Gambling Laws and Support

Gambling laws in Asia vary widely:

  • China: Most forms of gambling are illegal, with exceptions for state-run lotteries.
  • Japan: Recently legalized casino gambling in integrated resorts.
  • Singapore: Has strict gambling laws but allows casinos in integrated resorts.
  • Philippines: Has a thriving gambling industry, including online gambling.

Organizations that can help gamblers:

  • China: Chinese Association on Gambling Addiction - www.caga.org.cn
  • Japan: Be a Player - www.beaplayer.com
  • Singapore: National Council on Problem Gambling - www.ncpg.org.sg
  • Philippines: Philippine Council on Problem Gambling - www.pcpg.ph

Africa Gambling Laws and Support

Gambling laws in Africa are diverse and vary by country:

  • South Africa: Gambling is legal and regulated, with various forms including casinos and online betting.
  • Nigeria: Regulated gambling sector includes sports betting and online casinos.
  • Kenya: Some forms of gambling are legal, with restrictions based on licensing.

Organizations that can help gamblers:

  • South Africa: National Responsible Gambling Programme - www.ncpg.org.za
  • Nigeria: Gamblers Anonymous Nigeria - www.ganigeria.com
  • Kenya: The Gaming Commission of Kenya - www.gamingcommission.go.ke

Oceania Gambling Laws and Support

Gambling laws in Oceania vary significantly:

  • Australia: Each state regulates gambling, with various laws for online and land-based gambling.
  • New Zealand: Regulated gambling environment with legal betting outlets and online gambling.
  • Fiji: Gambling is legal but regulated with specific licenses required for operation.

Organizations that can help gamblers:

  • Australia: Gambling Help Online - www.gamblinghelponline.org.au
  • New Zealand: Problem Gambling Foundation of New Zealand - www.pgf.nz
  • Fiji: Fiji Ministry of Finance - www.finance.gov.fj
