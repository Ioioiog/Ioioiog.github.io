Signs of Problem Gambling

Recognizing the signs of problem gambling is crucial for early intervention and support. If you or someone you know exhibits several of these signs, it may be time to seek help.

Financial Warning Signs

  • Borrowing money for gambling
  • Selling possessions to fund gambling
  • Unexplained debt or loss of assets
  • Using funds for bills to gamble

Emotional Warning Signs

  • Anxiety or depression related to gambling
  • Mood swings or irritability
  • Feeling guilty or ashamed about gambling
  • Suicidal thoughts or attempts

Behavioral Warning Signs

  • Lying about gambling activities
  • Neglecting work or family responsibilities
  • Increasing time spent gambling
  • Unsuccessful attempts to cut back or quit

Social Warning Signs

  • Withdrawing from friends and family
  • Canceling social events to gamble
  • Lying to loved ones about gambling
  • Relationship problems due to gambling

What to Do If You Recognize These Signs

  1. Acknowledge the problem: Admitting there's an issue is the first step towards recovery.
  2. Seek support: Reach out to friends, family, or a professional counselor.
  3. Contact a helpline: Call the National Problem Gambling Helpline at 1-800-522-4700.
  4. Join a support group: Consider attending Gamblers Anonymous meetings.
  5. Self-exclude from gambling venues: Many casinos and online platforms offer self-exclusion programs.
  6. Explore treatment options: Look into cognitive-behavioral therapy or other specialized treatments.
